# Richard Millington — YouTube Transcript

## Video: Stop Chasing Engagement in Communities  
**Channel:** FeverBee  
**Link:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TCzc9qjOeXA  

## Summary  
Richard Millington explains why engagement is often misunderstood in community building and why focusing on value and outcomes is more important than maximizing activity.

## Key Insights

**1. Stop chasing engagement**  
Most companies try to maximize engagement, but this is not the right goal. Engagement cannot grow indefinitely and often reduces the overall user experience.

**2. More activity ≠ more value**  
High activity can overwhelm members and reduce content quality. A highly active community is not always a valuable one.

**3. Focus on optimal engagement**  
Instead of maximizing engagement, communities should aim for the right level of activity — enough to be useful, but not overwhelming.

**4. Community success = member outcomes**  
People join communities to solve problems, learn, and connect. Success should be measured by whether members achieve these outcomes.

**5. Quality > quantity**  
A smaller community with meaningful interactions and strong relationships is more valuable than a large but noisy one.

## My Take  
This shows that community-led growth should focus on delivering value rather than driving activity. By prioritizing meaningful outcomes over engagement metrics, companies can build more sustainable and impactful communities.
